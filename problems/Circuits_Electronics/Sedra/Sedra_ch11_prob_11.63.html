11.63 The CE BJT amplifier in Fig. P11.63 employs shunt–shunt feedback: Feedback resistor RF senses the output voltage Vo and provides a feedback current to the base node. (b) Find the small-signal equivalent circuit of the amplifier with the signal source represented by its Norton equivalent (as we usually do when the feedback connection at the input is shunt). (c) Find the A circuit and determine the value of A, Ri, and Ro. (d) Find β and hence Aβ and 1 + Aβ. (e) Find Af, Rif, and Rof and hence Rin and Rout. (f) What voltage gain Vo/Vs is realized? How does this value compare to the ideal value obtained if the loop gain is very large and thus the signal voltage at the base becomes almost zero (like what happens in an inverting op-amp circuit).
