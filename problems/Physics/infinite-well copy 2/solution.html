The Physics of Confinement

In this system, a particle is trapped in a 1D box of length $L$ with infinitely high walls. To find the solution, we solve the Time-Independent Schr√∂dinger Equation:

\[ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{d^2\psi(x)}{dx^2} = E\psi(x) \]

1. Boundary Conditions

Because the potential outside the well is infinite, the wavefunction must vanish at the walls to maintain continuity:

  • üîπ $\psi(0) = 0$
  • üîπ $\psi(L) = 0$

2. The General Solution

The solution to the differential equation is a sinusoidal wave. Applying the boundary condition at $x=L$, we find that the momentum $k$ must be quantized:

\[ \psi_n(x) = A \sin(kx) \implies k = \frac{n\pi}{L} \]

Where $n$ represents the quantum energy level $(1, 2, 3...)$

3. Normalized Wavefunction

The final normalized form ensures the particle exists somewhere within the well:

\[ \psi_n(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \]
