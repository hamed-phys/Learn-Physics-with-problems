Problem restatement

Two strings are joined at \(x=0\) by a massless ring that slides on a frictionless rod. String 1 lies on \(x<0\); string 2 lies on \(0<x<L\) and is fixed at \(x=L\). A right-moving triangular pulse of width \(L/4\) travels on string 1. At \(t=0\) the front edge is at \(-7L/8\).

Given quantities

  • \(\mu_1=\mu,\quad \mu_2=\mu/2\)
  • \(T_1=T,\quad T_2=T/2\)
  • Junction at \(x=0\): massless, frictionless ring (no inertia, no friction)
  • Wall at \(x=L\): fixed end \(y_2(L,t)=0\)
  • Incoming pulse: triangular, width \(L/4\), moving right

Unknowns

  • Displacements \(y_1(x,t)\) for \(x<0\), and \(y_2(x,t)\) for \(0<x<L\)
  • Reflected pulse function \(f_2\) on string 1
  • Transmitted pulse function \(g\) on string 2
  • Whether reflections occur at \(x=0\) and \(x=L\) and their sign

What must be found

  • Wave equations on both strings + boundary conditions at \(x=0\)
  • Reflection expectations + sign at \(x=0\) and \(x=L\)
  • Explicit \(f_2(x,t)\) and \(g(x,t)\) for \(t\le 2L\sqrt{\mu/T}\)
  • Sketches at \(t=L\sqrt{\mu/T}\) and \(t=2L\sqrt{\mu/T}\)

Relevant principles & why

Wave equation (small transverse vibrations): each string segment supports waves under tension: \(\;y_{tt}=v^2 y_{xx}\) with \(v=\sqrt{T/\mu}\).
Ring junction conditions: the connection enforces continuity of displacement and (because the ring is massless) transverse force balance using \(T\,y_x\).
Impedance mismatch controls reflection: reflection depends on \(Z=\sqrt{T\mu}\), not only wave speed.
Fixed-end reflection at the wall: \(y(L,t)=0\) forces an inverted reflected pulse.

Approach options & best choice

  • d’Alembert + boundary matching: write right/left waves and match at \(x=0\), then enforce \(y(L,t)=0\). (Best for explicit functions.)
  • Characteristic diagram: track wavefronts and apply coefficients at encounters. (Great for sketches/timing.)
  • Energy flux: relate transmitted/reflected energies, but still needs sign from boundary conditions.
Best approach: d’Alembert + boundary matching, because it produces functional forms directly and handles the special ring boundary cleanly.

Common mistakes & quick tips
  • Wrong direction: right-moving \(F(x/v-t)\), left-moving \(G(x/v+t)\). Keep signs consistent.

3 PART 3 — Full Solution

Physical intuition

Even if two strings share the same wave speed, a pulse can still partially reflect at the junction because reflection is governed by the string impedance \(Z=\sqrt{T\mu}\). Going from higher impedance to lower impedance produces a reflected pulse with opposite sign (partial inversion). At a truly fixed end (\(x=L\)), the reflection is fully inverted.

(a) Wave equations

Wave equation (each string)
\[ \frac{\partial^2 y_i}{\partial t^2}=v_i^2\frac{\partial^2 y_i}{\partial x^2}, \qquad v_i=\sqrt{\frac{T_i}{\mu_i}}. \]

Compute the speeds:

Wave speeds
\[ v_1=\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}},\qquad v_2=\sqrt{\frac{T/2}{\mu/2}}=\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}\equiv v. \]
Both strings have the same wave speed: \(v=\sqrt{T/\mu}\).

(a) Boundary conditions

Ring at x = 0
\[ y_1(0,t)=y_2(0,t) \] \[ T_1\,\frac{\partial y_1}{\partial x}(0^-,t)=T_2\,\frac{\partial y_2}{\partial x}(0^+,t) \]

Why the second condition? The ring is massless, so its net transverse force must vanish instantly. For small slopes, the transverse component of tension is approximately \(T\,y_x\).

Fixed end at x = L
\[ y_2(L,t)=0. \]

(b) Do we expect reflections? What is the sign?

The key quantity is the impedance: \(Z=\sqrt{T\mu}\).

Impedances
\[ Z_1=\sqrt{T\mu},\qquad Z_2=\sqrt{\frac{T}{2}\cdot\frac{\mu}{2}}=\sqrt{\frac{T\mu}{4}}=\frac{Z_1}{2}. \]

Because \(Z_2<Z_1\), the junction is a mismatch: yes, there will be reflection at \(x=0\). The displacement reflection coefficient is

Reflection and transmission at x=0 (displacement amplitudes)
\[ r=\frac{Z_2-Z_1}{Z_1+Z_2} =\frac{\frac{Z_1}{2}-Z_1}{Z_1+\frac{Z_1}{2}} =-\frac13, \qquad t=\frac{2Z_2}{Z_1+Z_2} =\frac{2}{3}. \]
At \(x=0\): reflected pulse has opposite sign (inverted), with amplitude factor \(-1/3\).
At \(x=L\): fixed end implies \(r_L=-1\), so reflection is fully inverted.

Before writing pulses: travel directions

  • Right-moving: \(F(x/v-t)\)
  • Left-moving: \(G(x/v+t)\)
We are given the incident pulse: \(f_1(x,t)=F(x/v-t)\).

(c) Functional forms of reflected and transmitted pulses (for \(t\le 2L\sqrt{\mu/T}\))

Decompose the motion as:

String 1 (x<0)
\[ y_1(x,t)=f_1(x,t)+f_2(x,t). \]

The reflected piece must be left-moving and satisfy \(f_2(0,t)=r\,f_1(0,t)\). Since \(f_1(0,t)=F(-t)\), a left-moving wave with the same time-trace at the boundary is \(F(-x/v-t)\). Therefore:

Reflected wave on string 1
\[ f_2(x,t)=r\,F\!\left(-\frac{x}{v}-t\right) =-\frac13\,F\!\left(-\frac{x}{v}-t\right), \qquad (x<0). \]

The transmitted wave on string 2 is right-moving with amplitude factor \(t\):

Transmitted wave on string 2 (before wall reflection)
\[ g(x,t)=t\,F\!\left(\frac{x}{v}-t\right)=\frac23\,F\!\left(\frac{x}{v}-t\right), \qquad (0<x<L). \]

To satisfy the fixed end at \(x=L\) within the time window \(t\le 2L/v\), we include only the first reflection from the wall (it has not yet returned to \(x=0\) by \(2L/v\)). A standard image construction that guarantees \(y_2(L,t)=0\) is:

String 2 including the first wall reflection
\[ y_2(x,t)=\frac23\,F\!\left(\frac{x}{v}-t\right) -\frac23\,F\!\left(\frac{2L-x}{v}-t\right), \qquad 0<x<L,\;\; t\le \frac{2L}{v}. \]
This form cancels at \(x=L\) because the two arguments coincide there, enforcing \(y_2(L,t)=0\) automatically.
Compact final result (core answer)
\[ v=\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}},\quad r=-\frac13,\quad t=\frac23 \] \[ y_1(x,t)=F\!\left(\frac{x}{v}-t\right)-\frac13\,F\!\left(-\frac{x}{v}-t\right)\quad (x<0) \] \[ y_2(x,t)=\frac23\,F\!\left(\frac{x}{v}-t\right)-\frac23\,F\!\left(\frac{2L-x}{v}-t\right)\quad (0<x<L,\;t\le 2L/v) \]

(d) Sketches at \(t=L\sqrt{\mu/T}\) and \(t=2L\sqrt{\mu/T}\)

Let \(t_1=\frac{L}{v}=L\sqrt{\mu/T}\) and \(t_2=\frac{2L}{v}=2L\sqrt{\mu/T}\). The incident triangular pulse has width \(L/4\). At \(t=0\) its front edge is at \(-7L/8\), so the back edge is at \(-9L/8\).

Edge tracking (timing logic)
  • Front reaches \(x=0\) at \(t=\frac{7L}{8v}\).
  • Back reaches \(x=0\) at \(t=\frac{9L}{8v}\).
  • At \(t=t_1=L/v\): front is at \(+L/8\), back is at \(-L/8\) (pulse straddles the junction).
  • At \(t=t_2=2L/v\): the wall reflection has begun and the right- and left-moving pieces on string 2 overlap near \(x\approx 7L/8\).

Qualitative sketch at \(t=t_1=L/v\)

incident transmitted reflected (inverted)

At \(t=L/v\) the incident triangle straddles the junction: part remains on string 1 and part has entered string 2. A smaller inverted reflected triangle on string 1 is already moving left with amplitude factor \(-1/3\).

Qualitative sketch at \(t=t_2=2L/v\)

transmitted on string 2 wall-reflected (inverted) reflected on string 1

By \(t=2L/v\) the transmitted pulse has reached the wall and a fully inverted reflection has started traveling left. Within this time window, the wall-reflected signal has not yet returned to the junction.

Sanity checks

Units

\(v=\sqrt{T/\mu}\): \(T\) in N \(=\text{kg·m/s}^2\), \(\mu\) in kg/m ⇒ \(T/\mu\) in m\(^2\)/s\(^2\) ⇒ \(v\) in m/s ✅

Arguments like \(\frac{x}{v}-t\) have units of time ✅

Limiting cases

  • If \(Z_2=Z_1\) ⇒ \(r=0\), \(t=1\): no reflection ✅
  • If \(Z_2\to 0\) ⇒ \(r\to -1\): fixed-end-like inversion ✅

Physical meaning

Equal wave speed does not eliminate reflection: impedance mismatch still redistributes energy between reflected and transmitted waves. The fixed end enforces a node, requiring inverted reflection.
