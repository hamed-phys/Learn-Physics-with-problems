Problem 4 — Normal Modes & Mode Amplitudes for a String with a Sliding Ring

A taut string of length \(L\), tension \(T\), and linear mass density \(\rho_L\) is initially held in a “step” shape and released from rest at \(t=0\). The left end is fixed; the right end passes through a massless ring that slides freely in the transverse direction. This article provides a complete, structured solution: analysis → roadmap → full derivation, including the unexcited modes.

PART 1 — Problem Analysis (no solving yet)

Restate the setup, list givens/unknowns, identify laws, compare approaches, choose the best.

30 pts

1 Restate the problem

We study transverse vibrations \(\psi(x,t)\) of a taut string (\(0\le x\le L\)). The string can move only along \(\hat y\) with small slope everywhere. The left end is clamped at a wall (\(x=0\)). The right end (\(x=L\)) is attached to a massless, frictionless ring that can slide up/down, so the end is not fixed in \(y\) but the ring cannot supply a transverse force. Gravity is ignored. The string is released from rest at \(t=0\) from the “step” shape: \[ \psi(x,0)=\begin{cases} 0, & 0\le x<\tfrac{5L}{7},\\ H, & \tfrac{5L}{7}\le x\le L, \end{cases} \qquad \dot{\psi}(x,0)=0. \] We must (a) write the boundary conditions, (b) give the 3 lowest normal modes and \(\omega_n\), (c) compute the amplitude \(a_n\) of the \(n^\text{th}\) mode for the initial shape, and (d) determine which modes are not excited.

2 Given, unknowns, and targets

Given
\(T,\ \rho_L,\ L,\ H;\) small-angle; gravity ignored; release from rest
Unknowns
\(\psi(x,t)\), BCs at \(0,L\), \(X_n(x)\), \(k_n\), \(\omega_n\), \(a_n\), unexcited \(n\)
Find/Prove
(a) BCs; (b) lowest 3 modes + \(\omega_n\); (c) \(a_n(H,n)\); (d) which \(a_n=0\)
Model
1D wave equation with mixed (Dirichlet/Neumann) boundary conditions

3 Principles & why they apply

  • Wave equation (taut string): Small slopes imply nearly constant tension magnitude \(T\) and linear dynamics, so \(\psi\) obeys \(\psi_{tt}=v^2\psi_{xx}\) with \(v=\sqrt{T/\rho_L}\).
  • Boundary conditions from constraints: the fixed wall enforces \(\psi(0,t)=0\). The ring is free to slide and massless, so it cannot sustain transverse force; thus the transverse component of tension at \(x=L\) must vanish, giving \(\psi_x(L,t)=0\).
  • Normal modes & orthogonality: linear PDE + linear BCs → Sturm–Liouville eigenfunctions. Expand the initial shape in these eigenfunctions to obtain \(a_n\).
Why does the ring imply \(\psi_x(L,t)=0\)? Tap to expand
At the endpoint, the string tension acts along the tangent. For small slope, the transverse force is \(F_y \approx T \sin\theta \approx T\tan\theta \approx T\,\psi_x\). A massless, frictionless ring cannot provide a net vertical reaction force, so \(F_y=0\) at the ring → \(\psi_x(L,t)=0\).

4 Candidate approaches & best choice

  • Separation of variables + eigenfunction expansion: systematic; directly yields \(X_n\), \(\omega_n\), and \(a_n\).
  • Method of images: can convert mixed BC to an extended Fourier series but is easier to misapply.
  • Pure symmetry arguments: can predict some missing modes but not the full coefficient formula.
Best approach: separation of variables + orthogonality (most rigorous and clean for parts a–d).

PART 2 — Strategy & Tips (roadmap only)

Minimal plan (5–10 steps), goals + key tools, plus common pitfalls.

Roadmap
  1. Write the PDE and wave speed — use \(\psi_{tt}=v^2\psi_{xx}\), \(v=\sqrt{T/\rho_L}\).
  2. Translate physical constraints into BCs — fixed end: \(\psi(0,t)=0\); ring/free end: \(\psi_x(L,t)=0\).
  3. Separate variables — assume \(\psi=X(x)G(t)\); obtain \(X''+k^2X=0\) and \(G''+\omega^2G=0\).
  4. Apply BCs to quantize \(k\) — enforce \(X(0)=0\), \(X'(L)=0\) → discrete \(k_n\).
  5. Write the normal-mode expansion — \(\psi=\sum (a_n\cos\omega_nt+b_n\sin\omega_nt)X_n\).
  6. Use “released from rest” — \(\dot\psi(x,0)=0\Rightarrow b_n=0\).
  7. Project initial shape — compute \(a_n\) via orthogonality: \(a_n\propto\int_0^L f(x)X_n(x)\,dx\).
  8. Identify unexcited modes — solve the integer condition when \(a_n=0\).

Common mistakes (avoid these)

• Don’t set \(\psi(L,t)=0\): the ring end is not fixed. • Mixed BCs produce half-integer modes \(k_nL=(2n-1)\pi/2\). • Normalize correctly: \(\int_0^L \sin^2(k_nx)\,dx=L/2\). • The step causes Gibbs overshoot near \(x=5L/7\) — normal and expected.

PART 3 — Full Solution

Intuition → detailed derivation → boxed results → sanity checks (units, limits, interpretation).

Derivation

Intuition What to expect physically

A fixed end at \(x=0\) forces a node: \(\psi(0,t)=0\). A frictionless sliding ring at \(x=L\) cannot exert a vertical reaction force, so the endpoint must have zero transverse force, which under small-slope conditions means zero slope: \(\psi_x(L,t)=0\). This fixed/free combination supports standing waves with a quarter-wave condition, producing half-integer \(k\) values. The initial displacement is a step, so many modes are excited, but some vanish by exact cancellation in the projection integral.

(a) Boundary conditions

Left end fixed: \[ \boxed{\psi(0,t)=0.} \] Right end on a massless, frictionless ring: transverse force must vanish. For small angles, \(F_y \approx T\,\psi_x(L,t)\). So \[ \boxed{\psi_x(L,t)=0.} \]

(b) Normal modes and the three lowest frequencies

The wave equation is \[ \psi_{tt}=v^2\psi_{xx},\qquad v=\sqrt{\frac{T}{\rho_L}}. \] Use \(\psi=X(x)G(t)\). Then \[ \frac{G''}{v^2G}=\frac{X''}{X}=-k^2 \quad\Rightarrow\quad X''+k^2X=0,\ \ G''+\omega^2G=0,\ \ \omega=vk. \] Solve \(X''+k^2X=0\Rightarrow X=A\sin(kx)+B\cos(kx)\). Apply \(X(0)=0\Rightarrow B=0\), so \(X=A\sin(kx)\). Apply \(X'(L)=0\Rightarrow Ak\cos(kL)=0\Rightarrow \cos(kL)=0\). Hence \[ kL=\left(n-\tfrac12\right)\pi,\quad n=1,2,3,\dots \] \[ \boxed{k_n=\frac{(2n-1)\pi}{2L}},\qquad \boxed{\omega_n=vk_n=\frac{(2n-1)\pi}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\rho_L}}} \] with mode shapes \[ \boxed{X_n(x)=\sin\!\left(\frac{(2n-1)\pi}{2L}x\right)}. \]
Three lowest mode shapes (as \(\psi\) vs. \(x\)) Tap to expand
\(n=1:\ X_1(x)=\sin(\pi x/2L)\), \(\omega_1=\frac{\pi}{2L}\sqrt{T/\rho_L}\).
\(n=2:\ X_2(x)=\sin(3\pi x/2L)\), \(\omega_2=\frac{3\pi}{2L}\sqrt{T/\rho_L}\).
\(n=3:\ X_3(x)=\sin(5\pi x/2L)\), \(\omega_3=\frac{5\pi}{2L}\sqrt{T/\rho_L}\).
Each has a node at \(x=0\) and zero slope at \(x=L\).

(c) Mode amplitudes \(a_n\) for the step initial shape

The initial shape from the figure is a step: the top segment has length \(2L/7\), so it occupies \(x\in[5L/7,\,L]\). Thus \[ f(x)=\psi(x,0)= \begin{cases} 0, & 0\le x<\tfrac{5L}{7},\\[4pt] H, & \tfrac{5L}{7}\le x\le L, \end{cases} \qquad \dot\psi(x,0)=0. \] Expand in eigenmodes: \[ \psi(x,t)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty\big[a_n\cos(\omega_nt)+b_n\sin(\omega_nt)\big]X_n(x). \] Since the string is released from rest, \(\dot\psi(x,0)=0\) implies \[ \boxed{b_n=0\ \text{for all }n.} \] So \(f(x)=\sum a_nX_n(x)\). Use orthogonality: \[ \int_0^L X_nX_m\,dx= \begin{cases} 0,&n\ne m,\\ \frac{L}{2},&n=m, \end{cases} \quad\text{for }X_n=\sin(k_nx). \] Multiply by \(X_n\) and integrate: \[ \int_0^L f(x)X_n(x)\,dx=a_n\int_0^L X_n^2(x)\,dx=a_n\frac{L}{2} \quad\Rightarrow\quad a_n=\frac{2}{L}\int_0^L f(x)\sin(k_nx)\,dx. \] Because \(f(x)=H\) only on \([5L/7,L]\), \[ a_n=\frac{2H}{L}\int_{5L/7}^{L}\sin(k_nx)\,dx =\frac{2H}{L}\left[\frac{-\cos(k_nx)}{k_n}\right]_{5L/7}^{L}. \] So \[ a_n=\frac{2H}{Lk_n}\Big(\cos(k_n\tfrac{5L}{7})-\cos(k_nL)\Big). \] But \(\cos(k_nL)=0\) from the quantization condition, hence \[ a_n=\frac{2H}{Lk_n}\cos\!\left(\frac{5k_nL}{7}\right). \] Substitute \(k_nL=\frac{(2n-1)\pi}{2}\) and \(Lk_n=\frac{(2n-1)\pi}{2}\): \[ \boxed{ a_n=\frac{4H}{(2n-1)\pi}\, \cos\!\left(\frac{5(2n-1)\pi}{14}\right) \qquad (n=1,2,3,\dots) }. \]

(d) Which modes are not excited?

A mode is not excited when \(a_n=0\), i.e. \[ \cos\!\left(\frac{5(2n-1)\pi}{14}\right)=0. \] This happens when the argument equals \(\frac{\pi}{2}+m\pi\): \[ \frac{5(2n-1)\pi}{14}=\left(m+\tfrac12\right)\pi \Rightarrow 5(2n-1)=14m+7. \] Solve: \[ 10n-5=14m+7 \Rightarrow 10n=14m+12 \Rightarrow 5n=7m+6. \] Integer solutions require \(m\equiv 2\ (\mathrm{mod}\ 5)\), so \(m=2+5p\), \(p=0,1,2,\dots\). Then \[ n=\frac{7(2+5p)+6}{5}=4+7p. \] Therefore \[ \boxed{\text{Unexcited modes: }n=4,\,11,\,18,\,25,\dots\ \ (\text{i.e. }n=4+7p).} \]

Sanity checks

• Units: \(v\) in m/s, \(\omega_n\) in 1/s, \(a_n\) in meters (same as \(H\)).
• Limits: \(H\to0\Rightarrow a_n\to0\). Large \(n\): \(a_n\sim 1/(2n-1)\) typical of a step.
• Interpretation: fixed end → node; sliding ring → zero slope (no transverse force).
