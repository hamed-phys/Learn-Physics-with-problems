PART 2 ‚Äî Strategy & Tips (Roadmap Only)

  1. State the operator: write ‚àáf = (‚àÇf/‚àÇx, ‚àÇf/‚àÇy, ‚àÇf/‚àÇz). Meaning: we need three components.
  2. Differentiate w.r.t x: treat y, z constants. Meaning: x-component gives sensitivity along x.
  3. Differentiate w.r.t y: treat x, z constants. Meaning: y-component gives sensitivity along y.
  4. Differentiate w.r.t z: treat x, y constants. Meaning: z-component gives sensitivity along z.
  5. Assemble vector: place components in (x,y,z) order. Meaning: a vector field in space.
  6. Sanity checks: units, limiting behavior, domain (ln z). Meaning: reduce algebra mistakes.
Common Mistakes (Quick Tips)
  • Mixing up component order (write x,y,z consistently).
  • For products: forgetting that only one factor depends on the variable you differentiate (others are constants).

  • Dropping factors like e^x or sin(y) when differentiating another factor.
