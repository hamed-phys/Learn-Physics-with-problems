Sensitivity of an Interferometric Electro-Optic Intensity Modulator

Sensitivity of a Mach–Zehnder Electro-Optic Intensity Modulator

Determine the incremental change of intensity transmittance per incremental change of voltage for a device with half-wave voltage Vπ = 10 V.
Contents Quick Summary Concept Primer Problem Analysis Strategy Full Solution Deeper Understanding

Quick Summary

  • The Mach–Zehnder modulator converts phase modulation into intensity modulation via interference.
  • Normalized intensity transmittance: T = (1 + cos Δφ)/2.
  • Electro-optic phase shift: Δφ(V) = πV / Vπ.
  • Sensitivity means slope dT/dV.
  • Maximum linear sensitivity occurs at quadrature bias (Δφ = π/2).
Final Result (Maximum Small-Signal Sensitivity) 
|dT/dV|_max = π / (2 Vπ) = π / (2 × 10 V) ≈ 0.157 V⁻¹

PART 0 — Concept Primer

Key Equations

T(Δφ) = (1 + cos Δφ)/2
Δφ(V) = π V / Vπ
The slope depends on operating point. Linear analog modulation implies quadrature bias (Δφ = π/2).

PART 1 — Problem Analysis

Given:
  • Vπ = 10 V
  • Ideal Mach–Zehnder interferometer
Find:
  • Sensitivity dT/dV

PART 2 — Strategy

  1. Write T as function of Δφ.
  2. Substitute Δφ(V).
  3. Differentiate using chain rule.
  4. Evaluate at quadrature.

PART 3 — Full Solution

From interference: 
T(V) = (1 + cos(πV/Vπ)) / 2
Differentiate: 
dT/dV = - (1/2) sin(πV/Vπ) · (π/Vπ)
At quadrature: 
sin(πV/Vπ) = 1
Therefore: 
|dT/dV| = π / (2 Vπ)
Substitute Vπ = 10 V: 
π / (2 × 10) ≈ 0.157 V⁻¹

PART 4 — Deeper Understanding

  • Sensitivity scales inversely with Vπ.
  • Lower Vπ ⇒ higher modulation efficiency.
  • The slope is zero at transmission maxima and minima.
  • Quadrature bias ensures linear operation.
Concept Check
  • Q: Where is sensitivity maximum? A: At Δφ = π/2.
  • Q: Units of dT/dV? A: V⁻¹.
