PART 1 ‚Äî Problem Analysis what‚Äôs given, what to find, best methods
Restate: Evaluate four integrals (some definite, some indefinite) using the cleanest method, showing reasoning and final answers.

Given

  • Four integrands with bounds for (a), (c), (d).
  • \(\ln(1)=0\).

Find

  • Numerical values for (a), (c), (d).
  • Antiderivative for (b).

Why these tools apply (physics mindset)

  • Substitution is a coordinate change: pick \(u=f(x)\) to simplify the ‚Äúmeasure‚Äù.
  • Derivative matching (\(f'(x)\) appears) is the designed shortcut in many energy/probability integrals.
  • Definite integrals represent accumulated totals (area, probability, work).

Best method per part

  • (a) \(u=\cos^2\theta\) because \(du=-2\sin\theta\cos\theta\,d\theta\).
  • (b) \(u=x^3+3x^2+1\) because \(du=3x(x+2)\,dx\).
  • (c) Bounds collapse: \(\ln(1)=0\Rightarrow\int_0^0(\cdot)=0\).
  • (d) \(u=x^2+1\) because \(du=2x\,dx\) and denominator is \((x^2+1)^3\).
PART 2 ‚Äî Strategy & Tips roadmap only (no heavy algebra)
  1. (a) Use \(u=\cos^2\theta\). Convert bounds. Integrate \(\int \frac{du}{1+u}\).
  2. (b) Use \(u=x^3+3x^2+1\). Replace \(x(x+2)\,dx\) by \(\frac{1}{3}du\). Integrate \(u^{-1/2}\).
  3. (c) Compute \(\ln(1)\) first. Same bounds ‚Üí integral is zero.
  4. (d) Simplify to \(x(x^2-1)\). Use \(u=x^2+1\). Convert to powers of \(u\). Apply bounds.

Common mistakes

  • Dropping the minus sign in \(d(\cos\theta)=-\sin\theta\,d\theta\) (part a).
  • Forgetting the factor \(3\) in \(du=3x(x+2)\,dx\) (part b).
  • Not converting bounds after substitution (parts a and d).
  • Overcomplicating (c) with special functions.
PART 3 ‚Äî Full Solution step-by-step derivations + checks
Intuition: Many physics integrals are ‚Äúbuilt‚Äù so that the numerator is the derivative of something in the denominator. That turns messy-looking expressions into logs or simple power rules.

(a) \(\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\pi/4}\frac{2\sin\theta\cos\theta}{1+\cos^2\theta}\,d\theta\)

Substitute \(u=\cos^2\theta\). Then \(du=-2\sin\theta\cos\theta\,d\theta\), so \(2\sin\theta\cos\theta\,d\theta=-du\).

\[ I_a=\int_{0}^{\pi/4}\frac{2\sin\theta\cos\theta}{1+\cos^2\theta}\,d\theta =\int_{\theta=0}^{\theta=\pi/4}\frac{-du}{1+u}. \]

Bounds: \(\theta=0\Rightarrow u=1\), \(\theta=\pi/4\Rightarrow u=\tfrac12\).

\[ I_a=\int_{1/2}^{1}\frac{1}{1+u}\,du =\Big[\ln(1+u)\Big]_{1/2}^{1} =\ln 2-\ln\!\left(\frac{3}{2}\right) =\ln\!\left(\frac{4}{3}\right). \]
\[ \boxed{I_a=\ln\!\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)} \]
Units: dimensionless ‚úÖ Integrand \(>0\) ‚áí result \(>0\) ‚úÖ

(b) \(\displaystyle \int \frac{x(x+2)}{\sqrt{x^3+3x^2+1}}\,dx\)

Substitute \(u=x^3+3x^2+1\). Then \(du=(3x^2+6x)\,dx=3x(x+2)\,dx\), so \(x(x+2)\,dx=\tfrac{1}{3}du\).

\[ I_b=\int \frac{x(x+2)}{\sqrt{x^3+3x^2+1}}\,dx =\frac{1}{3}\int u^{-1/2}\,du. \]
\[ \frac{1}{3}\int u^{-1/2}\,du =\frac{1}{3}\cdot 2u^{1/2}+C =\frac{2}{3}\sqrt{u}+C. \]
\[ \boxed{I_b=\frac{2}{3}\sqrt{x^3+3x^2+1}+C} \]
Differentiate to verify ‚úÖ Pattern: \(u'/\sqrt{u}\) ‚úÖ

(c) \(\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\ln(1)} e^{-t^2}\,dt\)

Compute the upper bound: \(\ln(1)=0\). So the interval has zero width.

\[ \boxed{I_c=\int_{0}^{0}e^{-t^2}\,dt=0} \]
Zero-width interval ‚áí zero accumulation ‚úÖ

(d) \(\displaystyle \int_{0}^{1}\frac{(x-1)(x+1)x}{(x^2+1)^3}\,dx\)

Simplify numerator: \((x-1)(x+1)=x^2-1\Rightarrow (x-1)(x+1)x=x(x^2-1)\).

Substitute \(u=x^2+1\Rightarrow du=2x\,dx\Rightarrow x\,dx=\tfrac12 du\), and \(x^2=u-1\Rightarrow x^2-1=u-2\).

Bounds: \(x=0\Rightarrow u=1\), \(x=1\Rightarrow u=2\).

\[ I_d=\int_{0}^{1}\frac{x(x^2-1)}{(x^2+1)^3}\,dx =\frac12\int_{1}^{2}\frac{u-2}{u^3}\,du =\frac12\int_{1}^{2}\left(u^{-2}-2u^{-3}\right)\,du. \]
\[ \int\left(u^{-2}-2u^{-3}\right)\,du =-u^{-1}+u^{-2}. \]
\[ I_d=\frac12\Big[-u^{-1}+u^{-2}\Big]_{1}^{2} =\frac12\left(\left[-\frac12+\frac14\right]-[ -1+1]\right) =\frac12\left(-\frac14\right) =-\frac18. \]
\[ \boxed{I_d=-\frac{1}{8}} \]
Integrand negative on \((0,1)\) ‚áí result negative ‚úÖ Units: dimensionless ‚úÖ
Pattern to remember: if you see \(f'(x)/f(x)\) or \(f'(x)/\sqrt{f(x)}\), try \(u=f(x)\).
