Riemann Sum → Integral Limit

Interpreting a discrete sum as a continuous integral and computing the exact value.

PART 1 — Problem Analysis

Problem:

\[ \lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\sin\!\left(\frac{k b}{n}\right), \quad b>0 \]
  • Given: constant \(b>0\), discrete sum of sine samples
  • Unknown: value of the limit
  • Goal: interpret as a Riemann sum and compute an integral

As \(n\to\infty\), discrete sampling becomes continuous. Therefore the sum should approach the average area under a smooth curve.

We expect:

\[ \text{Riemann sum} \;\rightarrow\; \text{definite integral} \]

PART 2 — Strategy & Tips

  • Define \(x_k=\frac{k}{n}\)
  • Recognize \(\Delta x=\frac{1}{n}\)
  • Rewrite the sum as \(\sum f(x_k)\Delta x\)
  • Convert to \(\int_0^1 f(x)dx\)
  • Integrate normally

Tip: any expression of the form \(\frac{1}{n}\sum f(k/n)\) is almost always an integral on \([0,1]\).

PART 3 — Full Solution

Step 1 — Rewrite the sum

\[ x_k=\frac{k}{n},\qquad \Delta x=\frac{1}{n} \] \[ \sin\!\left(\frac{k b}{n}\right)=\sin(bx_k) \]

Step 2 — Recognize the Riemann sum

\[ \sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\sin(bx_k)\Delta x \;\longrightarrow\; \int_0^1 \sin(bx)\,dx \]

Step 3 — Compute the integral

\[ \int_0^1 \sin(bx)\,dx = \left[-\frac{\cos(bx)}{b}\right]_0^1 = \frac{1-\cos b}{b} \]
\[ \boxed{\displaystyle \frac{1-\cos b}{b}} \]

Sanity Checks

  • Units: dimensionless → correct
  • Small \(b\): ≈ \(b/2\) (linear behavior)
  • Large \(b\): → 0 (oscillations cancel)
  • Physics view: average value of a sine signal
