Problem 3 — Tangent Line to an Implicit Curve at \((1,2)\)

Given the implicit relation \(\;2x^3-9xy+2y^3=0\;\) with \(y=f(x)\), and knowing the curve passes through \((1,2)\), we compute the slope \(y'(1,2)\) and build the tangent line.

Constraint curve: \(F(x,y)=0\) Tool: product + chain rules Output: point-slope line

Given

\(\;2x^3-9xy+2y^3=0\), and the point \((1,2)\) lies on the curve.

Find

The tangent line equation at \((1,2)\).

Key Output

Slope \(\;m=\dfrac45\), Line \(\;y-2=\dfrac45(x-1)\).

PART 1 — Problem Analysis (no solving yet) What’s given, what’s asked, and why implicit differentiation is the right tool.

Restate Problem in my words

A curve is defined implicitly by \(\;2x^3-9xy+2y^3=0\;\). Since \(y\) depends on \(x\), we must find the tangent line at the point \((1,2)\), which means computing the slope \(y'\) there and writing the line equation.

\[ 2x^3 - 9xy + 2y^3 = 0, \qquad (1,2)\ \text{on the curve} \]

Inventory Given / Unknown / Required

  • Given: \(2x^3-9xy+2y^3=0\), and \((x,y)=(1,2)\).
  • Unknowns: \(y'(x)\) and \(y'(1,2)\); the tangent line \(y=mx+b\).
  • Need to find: the tangent line equation at \((1,2)\).

Principles What applies (and why)

  • Implicit differentiation: \(y\) is not isolated; the relation couples \(x\) and \(y\).
  • Product rule: the term \(xy\) requires \(\frac{d}{dx}(xy)=x\,y' + y\).
  • Chain rule: \(y^3\) differentiates as \(\frac{d}{dx}(y^3)=3y^2y'\).
  • Tangent line form: once slope \(m\) is known, use \(y-y_0=m(x-x_0)\).

Approaches Options (quick compare)

  • Implicit diff (direct): fastest and standard; minimal algebra overhead.
  • Solve for \(y(x)\) explicitly: would require solving a cubic in \(y\); unnecessary.
  • Gradient method: treat \(F(x,y)=0\), use \(y'=-\frac{F_x}{F_y}\); equivalent and elegant.
Best choice: direct implicit differentiation — it’s the cleanest route to \(y'\) and the tangent line.

PART 3 — Full Solution Intuition → derivation → final boxed result + sanity checks.

Intuition “Constraint” & slope as a response rate

The equation \(\;2x^3-9xy+2y^3=0\;\) acts like a constraint: as \(x\) changes a tiny amount, \(y\) must change in just the right way to keep the constraint satisfied. That required “response rate” is the tangent slope \(y'=\frac{dy}{dx}\).

Derivation Implicit differentiation, step-by-step

\[ 2x^3 - 9xy + 2y^3 = 0 \]

Differentiate both sides with respect to \(x\). Remember: \(y=y(x)\).

\[ \frac{d}{dx}(2x^3) \;-\; 9\frac{d}{dx}(xy) \;+\; \frac{d}{dx}(2y^3) \;=\; 0 \]
  • Power rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}(2x^3)=6x^2\).
  • Product rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}(xy)=x\,y' + y\).
  • Chain rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}(2y^3)=6y^2y'\).
\[ 6x^2 \;-\; 9(xy' + y) \;+\; 6y^2y' = 0 \]

Distribute and collect the \(y'\) terms.

\[ 6x^2 - 9xy' - 9y + 6y^2y' = 0 \] \[ (-9x + 6y^2)\,y' + (6x^2 - 9y) = 0 \]

Solve for \(y'\) by isolating it:

\[ (-9x + 6y^2)\,y' = 9y - 6x^2 \] \[ y'=\frac{9y - 6x^2}{6y^2 - 9x} \]

Now evaluate at \((1,2)\):

\[ y'(1,2)=\frac{9(2)-6(1)^2}{6(2^2)-9(1)} =\frac{18-6}{24-9} =\frac{12}{15} =\frac{4}{5} \]

Use point-slope form at \((1,2)\) with slope \(m=\frac45\):

\[ y-2=\frac{4}{5}(x-1) \]

Final Answer (tangent line)

\[ \boxed{\,y-2=\frac45(x-1)\,} \]

Equivalent slope-intercept form: \(\;y=\frac45x+\frac65\).

Sanity checks Units • limits • interpretation

  • Units: no units given; treat \(x,y\) as dimensionless ⇒ slope \(dy/dx\) is dimensionless (consistent).
  • Limiting check: denominator at \((1,2)\) is \(6y^2-9x=15\neq0\), so the tangent is not vertical (slope finite).
  • Interpretation: near \((1,2)\), small changes obey \(\Delta y \approx \frac45\,\Delta x\) along the constraint curve.
