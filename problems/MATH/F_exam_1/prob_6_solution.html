PART 1 Problem Analysis (no solving yet)

Model → Variables → Tools

Restatement

Compute the derivative \(dy/dx\) for each of the following:

  • (a) \(\displaystyle y=\frac{e^{x}-e^{-x}}{e^{x}+e^{-x}}\)
  • (b) \(\displaystyle y=x\ln(x)-x\)
  • (c) \(\displaystyle y=\sqrt{1+x^{1234}}\)
  • (d) \(\displaystyle y=\log_{10}(x^{3}+3x)\)

Given / Unknown / Goal

  • Given: explicit formulas for \(y(x)\).
  • Unknowns: derivatives \(y'(x)\) for each part.
  • Goal: compute \( \dfrac{dy}{dx} \) and present clean final results.
Physics mindset: A derivative measures sensitivity—how fast \(y\) changes if \(x\) changes. Many physics quantities (velocity, acceleration, growth/decay rates) are derivatives.

Relevant rules (why they apply)

  • Quotient rule for a ratio \(N/D\) (part a).
  • Product rule for \(x\ln x\) (part b).
  • Chain rule for compositions like \(\sqrt{1+x^{1234}}\) (part c).
  • Log rule (base 10) + chain for \(\log_{10}(u(x))\) (part d).

Approaches (choose best)

  • Direct rules: always valid; may be algebra-heavy.
  • Simplify first: reduces errors (especially part a).
  • Identity-based: fastest if recognized (e.g., hyperbolic forms).
Best approach: simplify when beneficial, then apply the standard rule cleanly. This is robust and minimizes algebra mistakes.

PART 2 Strategy & Tips (roadmap only)

Plan (5–10 steps)

Roadmap (minimal)

  1. Recognize the expression type (ratio/product/power/log).
  2. Optionally simplify (e.g., exponentials or roots as powers).
  3. Select the correct differentiation rule.
  4. Differentiate the outer structure first (quotient/product/log).
  5. Differentiate the inner functions (chain rule components).
  6. Assemble the derivative and simplify.
  7. Check domain constraints (like \(\ln x\) needs \(x>0\)).
  8. Run sanity checks (limits, sign, qualitative behavior).

Common mistakes & quick tips

  • Exponentials: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}e^{-x}=-e^{-x}\) (don’t drop the minus).
  • Product rule: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}[x\ln x]=\ln x+1\).
  • Root → chain: \(\sqrt{u}=u^{1/2}\Rightarrow y'=\tfrac12u^{-1/2}u'\).
  • Base-10 log: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\log_{10}(u)=\dfrac{u'}{u\ln 10}\).
  • Domain: \(\ln x\) requires \(x>0\); \(\log_{10}(x^3+3x)\) requires \(x^3+3x>0\).

PART 3 Full Solution

Step-by-step derivations

Physical intuition (before the math)

In physics, derivatives represent rates of change (e.g., \(v=\frac{dx}{dt}\), \(a=\frac{dv}{dt}\)). Here, \(y'(x)\) tells you how rapidly \(y\) changes when \(x\) changes. Each part is a different “shape” of relationship (ratio, product, root, log), so we choose the matching rule.

Part (a)
\( \displaystyle y=\frac{e^{x}-e^{-x}}{e^{x}+e^{-x}} \)
GoalSet up quotient rule
\[ N(x)=e^{x}-e^{-x},\qquad D(x)=e^{x}+e^{-x}. \] \[ y=\frac{N}{D}\quad \Rightarrow\quad y'=\frac{N'D - ND'}{D^2}. \]
ToolDifferentiate \(N\), \(D\)
\[ N'(x)=\frac{d}{dx}(e^{x}-e^{-x})=e^{x}-(-e^{-x})=e^{x}+e^{-x}. \] \[ D'(x)=\frac{d}{dx}(e^{x}+e^{-x})=e^{x}+(-e^{-x})=e^{x}-e^{-x}. \]
AssembleApply quotient rule
\[ y'=\frac{(e^{x}+e^{-x})(e^{x}+e^{-x})-(e^{x}-e^{-x})(e^{x}-e^{-x})}{(e^{x}+e^{-x})^2}. \]
SimplifyUse \((A+B)^2-(A-B)^2=4AB\)
Let \(A=e^x\), \(B=e^{-x}\). Then \[ (A+B)^2-(A-B)^2=4AB=4(e^x)(e^{-x})=4. \] Therefore \[ y'=\frac{4}{(e^x+e^{-x})^2}. \]
Final Answer clean form
\[ \boxed{\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{4}{(e^x+e^{-x})^2}} \]
Units: exponentials require dimensionless \(x\) → derivative is dimensionless.
Limits: \(x\to\pm\infty\Rightarrow e^{-x}\) or \(e^{x}\) dominates → denominator grows → \(y'\to 0\).
Interpretation: a smooth saturating “switch” curve; steepest near \(x=0\), flat at extremes.
Part (b)
\( \displaystyle y=x\ln(x)-x \)
GoalDifferentiate \(x\ln x\)
Product rule: if \(h=fg\), then \(h'=f'g+fg'\). Let \(f=x\), \(g=\ln x\).
ToolCompute derivatives
\[ f'=1,\qquad g'=\frac{1}{x}. \] \[ \frac{d}{dx}(x\ln x)=1\cdot \ln x + x\cdot \frac{1}{x}=\ln x+1. \]
AssembleInclude \(-x\)
\[ \frac{d}{dx}(-x)=-1 \quad\Rightarrow\quad y'=(\ln x+1)-1=\ln x. \]
Final Answer domain: \(x>0\)
\[ \boxed{\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}=\ln(x)}\qquad (x>0) \]
Units: in physics, \(\ln(x)\) requires dimensionless \(x\).
Limiting case: at \(x=1\), \(y'=\ln 1=0\) (flat slope).
Interpretation: sensitivity grows slowly with \(x\) (logarithmically).
Part (c)
\( \displaystyle y=\sqrt{1+x^{1234}} \)
RewriteRoot as a power
\[ y=\sqrt{1+x^{1234}}=(1+x^{1234})^{1/2}. \]
GoalApply chain rule
Let \(u=1+x^{1234}\). Then \(y=u^{1/2}\). \[ \frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx}. \]
ToolDifferentiate pieces
\[ \frac{dy}{du}=\frac12 u^{-1/2},\qquad \frac{du}{dx}=1234x^{1233}. \]
AssembleMultiply + simplify
\[ y'=\frac12(1+x^{1234})^{-1/2}\cdot 1234x^{1233} =\frac{1234}{2}\frac{x^{1233}}{\sqrt{1+x^{1234}}} =617\,\frac{x^{1233}}{\sqrt{1+x^{1234}}}. \]
Final Answer chain rule
\[ \boxed{\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}=617\,\frac{x^{1233}}{\sqrt{1+x^{1234}}}} \]
Units: \(1+x^{1234}\) suggests \(x\) is dimensionless (unless scaled).
Limit: for large \(x>0\), \(\sqrt{1+x^{1234}}\sim x^{617}\Rightarrow y'\sim 617x^{616}\) (consistent).
Interpretation: extremely steep growth for large \(|x|\) due to high power.
Part (d)
\( \displaystyle y=\log_{10}(x^3+3x) \)
RuleBase-10 log derivative
\[ \frac{d}{dx}\log_{10}(u)=\frac{u'}{u\,\ln 10}. \] This comes from \(\log_{10}(u)=\dfrac{\ln u}{\ln 10}\).
GoalDifferentiate the inside
Let \(u=x^3+3x\). Then \[ u'=3x^2+3=3(x^2+1). \]
AssembleChain rule + simplify
\[ y'=\frac{u'}{u\ln 10} =\frac{3(x^2+1)}{(x^3+3x)\ln 10}. \]
Final Answer domain matters
\[ \boxed{\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{3(x^2+1)}{(x^3+3x)\,\ln 10}} \]
Units: logs require dimensionless arguments in physics; treat \(x\) as dimensionless unless scaled.
Domain: must have \(x^3+3x>0\) for real \(\log_{10}\).
Limit: large \(|x|\Rightarrow y'\approx \dfrac{3}{x\ln 10}\to 0\) (log grows slowly).

Final boxed results (all parts)

\[ \boxed{\text{(a)}\;\; y'=\frac{4}{(e^x+e^{-x})^2}} \qquad \boxed{\text{(b)}\;\; y'=\ln(x)} \] \[ \boxed{\text{(c)}\;\; y'=617\,\frac{x^{1233}}{\sqrt{1+x^{1234}}}} \qquad \boxed{\text{(d)}\;\; y'=\frac{3(x^2+1)}{(x^3+3x)\ln 10}} \]
