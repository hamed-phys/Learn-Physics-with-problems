Problem 2 — Tangent Lines to a Parabola

Calculus
Geometry
Tangents

The Problem

Parabola: \(\;y=x^2+2\).

Find the equations of the two tangent lines that pass through \(P=(0,1)\).

Extra credit: For tangency points \(Q=(x_0,x_0^2+2)\), \(R=(x_1,x_1^2+2)\) and intersection of tangents \(S=(x_2,y_2)\), prove \(2x_2=x_0+x_1\).

PART 1 — Problem Analysis (no solving yet)

Given
  • \(y=x^2+2\)
  • \(P=(0,1)\)
Unknowns
  • Two tangent line equations through \(P\)
Principles (why)
  • Slope of tangent at \(x=a\) is \(f'(a)\).
  • Tangent line: \(y-f(a)=f'(a)(x-a)\).
  • Pass-through constraint: substitute \(P\) into the tangent equation to solve for \(a\).
Approaches
  • ✅ Parameterize tangency point \(a\) (most direct)
  • Line through \(P\): \(y=mx+1\) + discriminant \(=0\)

PART 2 — Strategy & Tips (roadmap only)

  • Compute \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x)=x^2+2\).
  • Write the tangent at \(x=a\): \(y-(a^2+2)=2a(x-a)\).
  • Force it to pass through \(P=(0,1)\) and solve for \(a\).
  • Plug each \(a\) into the tangent formula → two lines.
  • Verify tangency via a double root check.
Common mistakes
  • Using \((a,a^2)\) instead of \((a,a^2+2)\).
  • Forgetting slope is \(2a\), not \(a\).
  • Expecting only one tangent (constraint gives a quadratic → two).

PART 3 — Full Solution

Physical intuition: \(P=(0,1)\) lies below the vertex \((0,2)\), so two symmetric tangents are expected.

Let \(f(x)=x^2+2\). Then \(f'(x)=2x\).

Tangent at \(x=a\) has slope \(2a\) and passes through \((a,a^2+2)\):

\[ y-(a^2+2)=2a(x-a). \]

Impose that \((0,1)\) lies on it:

\[ 1-(a^2+2)=2a(0-a). \]

Simplify:

\[ -a^2-1=-2a^2 \;\Rightarrow\; a^2-1=0 \;\Rightarrow\; a=\pm 1. \]

Now write both tangent lines:

\[ a=1:\quad y-3=2(x-1)\Rightarrow y=2x+1. \] \[ a=-1:\quad y-3=-2(x+1)\Rightarrow y=-2x+1. \]
Final Answers: \[ \boxed{\,y=2x+1\,}\qquad \boxed{\,y=-2x+1\,} \]
Quick verification
  • Both pass through \((0,1)\).
  • \(2x+1=x^2+2\Rightarrow (x-1)^2=0\) (double root → tangent).
  • \(-2x+1=x^2+2\Rightarrow (x+1)^2=0\) (double root → tangent).

Extra Credit — Intersection of Tangents Bisects the Chord

At \(x=x_0\) the tangent is:

\[ y-(x_0^2+2)=2x_0(x-x_0)\Rightarrow y=2x_0x-x_0^2+2. \]

At \(x=x_1\):

\[ y=2x_1x-x_1^2+2. \]

At the intersection \(S\), set them equal:

\[ 2x_0x-x_0^2+2=2x_1x-x_1^2+2 \Rightarrow 2(x_0-x_1)x=(x_0-x_1)(x_0+x_1). \]

Since \(x_0\neq x_1\), divide by \((x_0-x_1)\):

\[ 2x=x_0+x_1. \]

At the intersection \(x=x_2\), so:

\[ \boxed{\,2x_2=x_0+x_1\,} \]
