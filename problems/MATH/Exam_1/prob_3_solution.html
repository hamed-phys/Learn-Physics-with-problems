Problem 3 — Tangent Line via Implicit Differentiation

Implicit Differentiation
Tangent Line

The Problem

Ellipse (implicit):

\[ 9(x+y)^2+(x-y)^2=36 \]

Point on the curve:

\[ T=\left(-\frac{4\sqrt{10}}{5},\,\sqrt{10}\right) \]

Find the full equation of the tangent line at \(T\).

PART 1 — Problem Analysis (no solving yet)

Given
  • \(9(x+y)^2+(x-y)^2=36\)
  • \(T=\left(-\frac{4\sqrt{10}}{5},\sqrt{10}\right)\)
Unknown
  • Tangent line equation at \(T\)
Principles (why)
  • The curve is not \(y=f(x)\), so use implicit differentiation.
  • Chain rule for \((x\pm y)^2\): derivative brings \((1\pm y')\).
  • After finding \(y'(x)\), plug the point to get slope and then line.
Approaches
  • ✅ Implicit differentiation (matches requirement)
  • Gradient/normal method (fast but must connect to implicit work)

PART 2 — Strategy & Tips (roadmap only)

  • Differentiate both sides of \(9(x+y)^2+(x-y)^2=36\) w.r.t. \(x\).
  • Use chain rule: \(d/dx(x+y)=1+y'\), \(d/dx(x-y)=1-y'\).
  • Collect terms and solve for \(y'\).
  • Evaluate \(y'\) at \(T\) to get the slope \(m\).
  • Write tangent line through \(T\): \(y-y_T=m(x-x_T)\).
Common mistakes
  • Treating \(y\) as constant (forgetting \(y'\)).
  • Wrong chain rule signs: \(1+y'\) vs \(1-y'\).
  • Giving slope only, not the full line equation.

PART 3 — Full Solution

Physical intuition: The tangent is the best local linear approximation to a constraint curve (like linearizing an orbit/trajectory near a point).

Start from:

\[ 9(x+y)^2+(x-y)^2=36. \]

Differentiate both sides w.r.t. \(x\) (right side becomes 0):

\[ 9\cdot 2(x+y)\frac{d}{dx}(x+y) + 2(x-y)\frac{d}{dx}(x-y)=0. \]

Use \(\frac{d}{dx}(x+y)=1+y'\) and \(\frac{d}{dx}(x-y)=1-y'\):

\[ 18(x+y)(1+y') + 2(x-y)(1-y')=0. \]

Expand and group terms:

\[ \big(18(x+y)+2(x-y)\big) + \big(18(x+y)-2(x-y)\big)y'=0. \]

Solve for \(y'\):

\[ y'=-\frac{18(x+y)+2(x-y)}{18(x+y)-2(x-y)}. \]

Simplify numerator/denominator:

\[ 18(x+y)+2(x-y)=20x+16y,\qquad 18(x+y)-2(x-y)=16x+20y, \] \[ \Rightarrow\quad y'=-\frac{20x+16y}{16x+20y}. \]

Evaluate at \(T=\left(-\frac{4\sqrt{10}}{5},\sqrt{10}\right)\):

\[ 20x_T+16y_T =20\left(-\frac{4\sqrt{10}}{5}\right)+16(\sqrt{10}) =-16\sqrt{10}+16\sqrt{10}=0. \]

Denominator:

\[ 16x_T+20y_T =16\left(-\frac{4\sqrt{10}}{5}\right)+20(\sqrt{10}) =-\frac{64\sqrt{10}}{5}+\frac{100\sqrt{10}}{5} =\frac{36\sqrt{10}}{5}\neq 0. \]

So:

\[ y'(T)=-\frac{0}{36\sqrt{10}/5}=0. \]

Therefore the tangent line is horizontal through \(y=\sqrt{10}\):

Final Answer: \[ \boxed{\,y=\sqrt{10}\,} \]
Sanity checks
  • Slope \(0\) means the tangent is horizontal — plausible for an ellipse at a “top/bottom”-type point.
  • Normal direction would be vertical there (constraint force idea), consistent with tangent ⟂ normal.
