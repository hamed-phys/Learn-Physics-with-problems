Problem 4 — Stone Dropped from a Cliff

Kinematics
Derivatives
Physics

The Problem

The height (meters) after \(t\) seconds is:

\[ s(t)=80-5t^2. \]

(a) Find the velocity \(v_f\) at the instant it hits the ground.

(b) Find the height when the velocity equals \(\frac{1}{2}v_f\).

PART 1 — Problem Analysis (no solving yet)

Given
  • \(s(t)=80-5t^2\) (meters)
  • Ground at \(s=0\)
Unknowns
  • Impact time \(t_f\)
  • Impact velocity \(v_f=v(t_f)\)
  • Height when \(v=\frac12 v_f\)
Principles (why)
  • Velocity is derivative: \(v(t)=\frac{ds}{dt}\).
  • Impact occurs when \(s(t_f)=0\) with \(t_f>0\).
  • This matches constant acceleration: \(s(t)=s_0+v_0 t-\frac12 g t^2\) (here \(\frac12 g=5\Rightarrow g\approx 10\)).
Approaches
  • ✅ Differentiate \(s(t)\), solve for times (best)
  • Kinematics formulas / energy method (works, but given \(s(t)\) makes calculus simplest)

PART 2 — Strategy & Tips (roadmap only)

  • Solve \(s(t)=0\) for \(t>0\) to find \(t_f\).
  • Differentiate \(s(t)\) to get \(v(t)\).
  • Compute \(v_f=v(t_f)\).
  • Set \(v(t)=\frac12 v_f\) and solve for \(t\).
  • Plug that \(t\) into \(s(t)\) to get the height.
  • Check units and signs (falling → negative velocity).
Common mistakes
  • Using speed (magnitude) instead of velocity (signed).
  • Choosing the negative time root.
  • Forgetting that \(\frac12 v_f\) is still negative because \(v_f<0\).

PART 3 — Full Solution

Physical intuition: Height decreases like \(t^2\) under constant gravity, and velocity becomes more negative linearly in time.

(a) Velocity at impact

Impact happens when \(s(t)=0\):

\[ 80-5t^2=0 \Rightarrow 5t^2=80 \Rightarrow t^2=16 \Rightarrow t=\pm 4. \]

Since \(t>0\), the impact time is:

\[ t_f=4\text{ s}. \]

Velocity is \(v(t)=\frac{ds}{dt}\):

\[ v(t)=\frac{d}{dt}(80-5t^2)= -10t. \]

So the impact velocity is:

\[ v_f=v(4)=-10(4)=-40\ \text{m/s}. \]
Answer (a): \[ \boxed{\,v_f=-40\ \text{m/s}\,} \]

(b) Height when \(v=\frac12 v_f\)

Compute \(\frac12 v_f\):

\[ \frac12 v_f=\frac12(-40)=-20\ \text{m/s}. \]

Solve \(v(t)=-20\):

\[ -10t=-20 \Rightarrow t=2\ \text{s}. \]

Now compute the height at \(t=2\):

\[ s(2)=80-5(2^2)=80-5(4)=80-20=60\ \text{m}. \]
Answer (b): \[ \boxed{\,s=60\ \text{m}\,} \]
Sanity checks
  • Units: \(s\) in meters, \(v\) in m/s, consistent.
  • Signs: \(v(t)=-10t\) is negative for \(t>0\) → falling.
  • Reasonableness: at \(t=2\) (half the impact time), height is \(60\) (still above the midpoint 40) because distance fallen scales like \(t^2\).
