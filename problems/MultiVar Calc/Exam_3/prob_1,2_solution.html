PART 1 — Problem Analysis (no solving yet)

Concept Map

1) Restate the problems

  • Problem 1: For \(f(x,y)=xy-x^4\), (a) compute \(\nabla f\) at \(P=(1,1)\); (b) write a linear (differential) approximation for \(\Delta w\) near \((1,1)\).
  • Problem 2: Given a contour map for \(h(x,y)\) (feet) with adjacent contours differing by \(100\ \text{ft}\) and a distance scale: (a) estimate \(\left.\dfrac{dh}{ds}\right|_P\) in direction of \(\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j}\); (b) pick a point \(Q\) with \(h=2200,\ h_x=0,\ h_y<0\) and estimate \(h_y(Q)\).

2) Given vs. unknown

  • Given: \(f(x,y)=xy-x^4\), point \(P=(1,1)\).
  • Given: contour interval \(\Delta h=100\ \text{ft}\), map scale bar, point \(P\), direction \(\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j}\).
  • Unknown: \(\nabla f(1,1)\), linear approximation for \(\Delta w\), directional derivative at \(P\), and an estimate of \(h_y(Q)\).

3) Key principles (why they apply)

  • Gradient: \(\nabla f=\langle f_x,f_y\rangle\) gives steepest increase and feeds directional change via dot product.
  • Linearization: small changes propagate as \(\Delta w\approx f_x\Delta x+f_y\Delta y\).
  • Contour physics: each contour crossing is \(\pm 100\ \text{ft}\). Local slope is \(\Delta h/\Delta s\) along a direction.
  • Partial derivative meaning: \(h_x=0\) means no change when moving in \(x\) (level curve tangent horizontal), and \(h_y<0\) means height decreases as \(y\) increases.

4) Approaches (compare + choose)

  • Problem 1: Direct partial differentiation (exact, fastest).
  • Problem 2: Use contour spacing + scale (most direct; matches how topographic maps encode slope).

Common mistakes & quick tips

  • Forgetting to make \(\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j}\) a unit vector.
  • Using a non-local spacing between contours (use the nearest contour step near the point).
  • Sign errors: moving toward higher contours \(\Rightarrow\) positive derivative; toward lower \(\Rightarrow\) negative.
  • Mixing “screen up” and “\(+y\)” — by convention, \(\mathbf{j}\) points upward on the page.

Problem 1 — Gradient & Linearization

Field intuition: Think of \(f(x,y)\) as a scalar field (like potential). The gradient \(\nabla f\) points in the direction of steepest increase and encodes how sensitive \(f\) is to small changes in \(x\) and \(y\).

Given \(\; f(x,y)=xy-x^4\).

1

Compute \(f_x\). Treat \(y\) as constant:

\[ f_x(x,y)=\frac{\partial}{\partial x}(xy)-\frac{\partial}{\partial x}(x^4) =y-4x^3. \]
2

Compute \(f_y\). Treat \(x\) as constant:

\[ f_y(x,y)=\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(xy)-\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(x^4) =x. \]
3

Build the gradient.

\[ \nabla f(x,y)=\langle f_x,f_y\rangle=\langle y-4x^3,\ x\rangle. \]
4

Evaluate at \(P=(1,1)\).

\[ \nabla f(1,1)=\langle 1-4(1)^3,\ 1\rangle=\langle -3,\ 1\rangle. \]

Final result Problem 1(a)

\[ \boxed{\nabla f(1,1)=\langle -3,\ 1\rangle} \]
5

Linear (differential) approximation. For \(w=f(x,y)\), small changes satisfy

\[ \Delta w \approx f_x(1,1)\,\Delta x + f_y(1,1)\,\Delta y. \]

Substitute \(f_x(1,1)=-3,\; f_y(1,1)=1\).

\[ \Delta w \approx -3\,\Delta x + \Delta y. \]

Final result Problem 1(b)

\[ \boxed{\Delta w \approx -3\,\Delta x + \Delta y \quad \text{(near }(1,1)\text{)}} \]

Sanity checks

  • Sign: \(f_x(1,1)=-3\Rightarrow\) increasing \(x\) decreases \(f\) locally.
  • Direction test: If \(\Delta y=0\), then \(\Delta w\approx -3\Delta x\); if \(\Delta x=0\), \(\Delta w\approx \Delta y\).

Problem 2 — Topographic Directional Derivatives

Field intuition: \(h(x,y)\) is elevation (feet). Contours are level sets: each line is constant \(h\). The steepness in a direction is “rise over run”: \(\Delta h / \Delta s\).

Map facts: adjacent contours differ by \(100\ \text{ft}\). A 1000-ft scale bar is provided for distance conversion.

1

(a) Convert \(\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j}\) to a unit direction.

\[ \mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j}=\langle 1,1\rangle,\qquad \hat{\mathbf{u}}=\frac{\langle 1,1\rangle}{\sqrt{1^2+1^2}} =\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\langle 1,1\rangle. \]

Moving along \(\hat{\mathbf{u}}\) means moving northeast at 45°.

2

(a) Estimate rise \(\Delta h\) and run \(\Delta s\) near \(P\).

Along the northeast direction from \(P\), the next contour crossing corresponds to a change of \(\Delta h \approx +100\ \text{ft}\) (toward higher contours near the hill).

Using the 1000-ft scale bar, the measured distance from \(P\) to the next contour along that direction is approximately \(\Delta s \approx 7.0\times 10^2\ \text{ft}\) (about \(702\ \text{ft}\)).

3

(a) Compute directional derivative as slope.

\[ \left.\frac{dh}{ds}\right|_{P,\hat{\mathbf{u}}} \approx \frac{\Delta h}{\Delta s} =\frac{100\ \text{ft}}{702\ \text{ft}} \approx 0.1425. \]
\[ \boxed{\left.\frac{dh}{ds}\right|_P \approx 0.1\ \text{ft/ft (to nearest 0.1)}} \]

Sanity checks for (a)

  • Units: \(\text{ft}/\text{ft}\) (dimensionless slope), correct.
  • Sign: positive because the direction heads toward higher elevations.
  • Meaning: about 14 ft gained per 100 ft traveled (moderate slope).
4

(b) Choose a point \(Q\) with \(h=2200,\; h_x=0,\; h_y<0\).

Pick \(Q\) on the 2200-ft contour where the contour is locally horizontal. A horizontal tangent means changing \(x\) (moving left/right) stays on the same contour to first order, so \(h_x(Q)=0\).

To enforce \(h_y(Q)<0\): moving upward (in \(+y\)) should go to lower elevation. This occurs on the upper side of the hill’s 2200 contour.

5

(b) Estimate \(h_y(Q)\) by “vertical rise/run”.

Moving upward from \(h=2200\) to the next contour \(h=2100\) gives \(\Delta h = 2100-2200=-100\ \text{ft}\). Measure the vertical spacing \(\Delta y\) between these contours near \(Q\) using the scale: \(\Delta y \approx 1.6\times 10^2\ \text{ft}\) (about \(159\ \text{ft}\)).

\[ h_y(Q)\approx \frac{\Delta h}{\Delta y} =\frac{-100\ \text{ft}}{159\ \text{ft}} \approx -0.63. \]
\[ \boxed{\left.\frac{\partial h}{\partial y}\right|_Q \approx -0.6\ \text{ft/ft (to nearest 0.1)}} \]

Sanity checks for (b)

  • Units: \(\text{ft}/\text{ft}\), correct for a slope component.
  • Sign: negative matches \(h_y<0\).
  • Interpretation: per 1 ft northward, elevation drops about 0.6 ft near \(Q\) (steeper than at \(P\)).

Final results Problem 2

\[ \boxed{\left.\frac{dh}{ds}\right|_P \approx 0.1\ \text{ft/ft}} \] \[ \boxed{\text{Choose }Q\text{ on the top of the }2200\text{ contour (horizontal tangent), and } \left.\frac{\partial h}{\partial y}\right|_Q \approx -0.6\ \text{ft/ft}} \]
