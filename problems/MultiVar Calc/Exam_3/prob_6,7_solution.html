PART 1

Problem 6 — Restatement

Let \(w=f(u,v)\) where \(u=xy\) and \(v=\dfrac{x}{y}\). Use the multivariable chain rule to express \(\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial x}\) and \(\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial y}\) in terms of \(x,y,f_u,f_v\).

Given / Unknown / Find

  • Given: \(w=f(u,v)\), \(u=xy\), \(v=x/y\).
  • Unknowns: \(w_x=\partial w/\partial x\), \(w_y=\partial w/\partial y\).
  • Find: formulas for \(w_x,w_y\) using \(f_u,f_v,x,y\).

Problem 7 — Restatement

Suppose \(x^2y + xz^2 = 5\) and let \(w=x^3y\). Compute \(\left(\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial z}\right)_y\) as a function of \(x,y,z\), then evaluate at \((x,y,z)=(1,1,2)\).

Given / Unknown / Find

  • Given: constraint \(x^2y+xz^2=5\), function \(w=x^3y\).
  • Unknown: \(\left(\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial z}\right)_y\).
  • Find: symbolic expression and numerical value at \((1,1,2)\).

Relevant principles (and why)

  • Multivariable chain rule: \(w\) depends on \(x,y\) through intermediate variables \(u(x,y), v(x,y)\).
  • Implicit differentiation: In Problem 7, \(x\) is linked to \(z\) by the constraint; holding \(y\) fixed makes \(x=x(z)\).

Approaches & best choice

  • Direct chain rule (fastest, standard): ideal for Problem 6.
  • Total differential \(dw=f_u\,du+f_v\,dv\): equally clean for Problem 6.
  • Explicitly solve the constraint for \(x(z)\): possible but algebra-heavy.
Best approach: Use the chain rule (Problem 6) and implicit differentiation to get \((dx/dz)_y\) followed by the chain rule for \(w(x,y)\) (Problem 7).

PART 2 — Strategy & Tips (roadmap only)

Minimal plan (no algebra yet): what to compute, which tool to use, and how to avoid common mistakes.

PART 2

Roadmap (5–10 steps)

  1. Recognize dependence structure.
    Goal: write \(w=f(u(x,y),v(x,y))\). Tool: multivariable chain rule.
  2. Compute \(u_x,u_y,v_x,v_y\).
    Goal: derivatives of intermediate variables. Tool: partial differentiation.
  3. Build \(w_x\).
    Goal: \(w_x=f_u u_x + f_v v_x\). Tool: chain rule.
  4. Build \(w_y\).
    Goal: \(w_y=f_u u_y + f_v v_y\). Tool: chain rule.
  5. Differentiate the constraint wrt \(z\) at fixed \(y\).
    Goal: get \((dx/dz)_y\). Tool: implicit differentiation of \(F(x,y,z)=0\).
  6. Differentiate \(w=x^3y\) wrt \(z\) at fixed \(y\).
    Goal: \((\partial w/\partial z)_y = w_x (dx/dz)_y\). Tool: chain rule.
  7. Evaluate at \((1,1,2)\).
    Goal: numerical result. Tool: substitution + arithmetic.
Common mistakes
  • Swapping \(f_u\) and \(f_v\) (keep consistent with \(u,v\)).
  • Forgetting \(v=x/y\) depends on both \(x\) and \(y\).
  • In Problem 7, \(\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial z}\right)_y\) means \(y\) fixed but \(x\) changes.
Quick tips
  • Use the template: \(w_x=f_u u_x + f_v v_x\), \(w_y=f_u u_y + f_v v_y\).
  • For implicit constraints: \(F_x (dx/dz)_y + F_z = 0\Rightarrow (dx/dz)_y=-F_z/F_x\).
  • Always sanity-check signs: if the constraint forces \(x\) down as \(z\) rises, derivatives should reflect that.

PART 3 — Full Solution

Start with intuition, then a careful step-by-step derivation, ending with boxed results and sanity checks (units, limits, interpretation).

PART 3

Physical intuition

In physics, quantities often depend on “hidden” variables (state variables). The chain rule tells you how a small change in a measured variable (like \(x\) or \(z\)) propagates through intermediate relationships to change the final quantity \(w\). A constraint (Problem 7) acts like a physical condition: changing one variable forces another to adjust.

Problem 6 — Chain rule for \(w=f(u,v)\), \(u=xy\), \(v=x/y\)

We are given:

$$w=f(u,v),\qquad u=xy,\qquad v=\frac{x}{y}.$$

Multivariable chain rule:

$$\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}=f_u\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}+f_v\frac{\partial v}{\partial x},\qquad \frac{\partial w}{\partial y}=f_u\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}+f_v\frac{\partial v}{\partial y}.$$

Step 1: Compute partials of \(u\) and \(v\).

$$u_x=\frac{\partial}{\partial x}(xy)=y,\qquad u_y=\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(xy)=x.$$
$$v_x=\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(\frac{x}{y}\right)=\frac{1}{y},\qquad v_y=\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left(\frac{x}{y}\right)=x\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(y^{-1}) =x(-y^{-2})=-\frac{x}{y^2}.$$

Step 2: Substitute into the chain rule.

$$\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}=f_u(y)+f_v\left(\frac{1}{y}\right),\qquad \frac{\partial w}{\partial y}=f_u(x)+f_v\left(-\frac{x}{y^2}\right).$$
Final results (Problem 6)
$$\boxed{\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}=y\,f_u+\frac{1}{y}\,f_v}$$ $$\boxed{\frac{\partial w}{\partial y}=x\,f_u-\frac{x}{y^2}\,f_v}$$
Sanity checks (Problem 6)
  • Limiting case: If \(f\) depends only on \(u\) (\(f_v=0\)), then \(w_x=yf_u\) and \(w_y=xf_u\), matching \(u=xy\).
  • Structure: Each derivative is a sum of two “paths”: changing \(x\) or \(y\) affects both \(u\) and \(v\), and those effects add.

Problem 7 — Implicit differentiation + chain rule

Given the constraint and the function:

$$x^2y + xz^2 = 5,\qquad w=x^3y.$$

We want \(\left(\dfrac{\partial w}{\partial z}\right)_y\): vary \(z\) while holding \(y\) fixed. Then \(x\) must adjust with \(z\) to keep the constraint true.

Step 1: Differentiate the constraint to find \(\left(\dfrac{dx}{dz}\right)_y\).

Define

$$F(x,y,z)=x^2y + xz^2 - 5=0.$$

Differentiate with respect to \(z\) holding \(y\) constant:

$$\frac{dF}{dz}=F_x\left(\frac{dx}{dz}\right)_y + F_z = 0.$$

Compute partial derivatives:

$$F_x=\frac{\partial}{\partial x}(x^2y+xz^2-5)=2xy+z^2,$$ $$F_z=\frac{\partial}{\partial z}(x^2y+xz^2-5)=2xz.$$

Substitute into the implicit relation:

$$(2xy+z^2)\left(\frac{dx}{dz}\right)_y + 2xz = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \left(\frac{dx}{dz}\right)_y = -\frac{2xz}{2xy+z^2}.$$

Step 2: Differentiate \(w=x^3y\) with respect to \(z\) at fixed \(y\).

Since \(w\) has no explicit \(z\)-dependence, the change comes through \(x(z)\):

$$\left(\frac{\partial w}{\partial z}\right)_y=\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}\left(\frac{dx}{dz}\right)_y.$$

Compute \(\partial w/\partial x\) (treating \(y\) constant):

$$\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}=\frac{\partial}{\partial x}(x^3y)=3x^2y.$$

Therefore:

$$\left(\frac{\partial w}{\partial z}\right)_y = (3x^2y)\left(-\frac{2xz}{2xy+z^2}\right) = -\frac{6x^3yz}{2xy+z^2}.$$
Symbolic result (Problem 7)
$$\boxed{\left(\frac{\partial w}{\partial z}\right)_y = -\frac{6x^3yz}{2xy+z^2}}$$

Step 3: Numerical evaluation at \((x,y,z)=(1,1,2)\).

$$\text{Numerator: }-6x^3yz=-6(1)^3(1)(2)=-12,$$ $$\text{Denominator: }2xy+z^2=2(1)(1)+2^2=2+4=6,$$ $$\Rightarrow\left(\frac{\partial w}{\partial z}\right)_y=\frac{-12}{6}=-2.$$
Numerical value
$$\boxed{\left(\frac{\partial w}{\partial z}\right)_y\bigg|_{(1,1,2)}=-2}$$
Sanity checks (Problem 7)
  • Sign / physical interpretation: Increasing \(z\) increases the term \(xz^2\) in the constraint, so \(x\) must decrease to keep \(x^2y+xz^2\) fixed. Since \(w=x^3y\), decreasing \(x\) decreases \(w\), hence the derivative is negative — consistent with \(-2\).
  • Limiting case \(z\to 0\): \(\left(\frac{dx}{dz}\right)_y=-\frac{2xz}{2xy+z^2}\to 0\), so \(\left(\frac{\partial w}{\partial z}\right)_y\to 0\). When \(z\) is near zero, changing \(z\) barely affects the constraint.
  • Units: The expression combines \(x,y,z\) in the same way the constraint does; the ratio keeps dimensions consistent for a derivative “per \(z\)”.
Summary: Problem 6 is a direct two-path chain rule. Problem 7 uses the constraint to compute \((dx/dz)_y\), then chains into \(w=x^3y\).
